Level 1: Foundation (Beginner)
Step 1: The Alphabet & Pronunciation
  • Learn the 26 letters (uppercase and lowercase)
  • Master basic pronunciation sounds (vowels: a, e, i, o, u and consonants)
  • Practice phonetics to understand how words sound
  • Learn common letter combinations (th, ch, sh, ph)
Step 2: Basic Vocabulary (500-1000 words)
  • Start with everyday words: numbers, colors, days, months, family members
  • Learn common nouns (things you see daily)
  • Basic verbs (eat, sleep, walk, talk, go, come)
  • Simple adjectives (big, small, good, bad, hot, cold)
  • Use flashcards or vocabulary apps on your Android phone
Step 3: Simple Grammar Foundations
  • Understand parts of speech: nouns, verbs, adjectives, pronouns
  • Learn subject-verb agreement (I am, you are, he is)
  • Present simple tense (I eat, she walks)
  • Articles: a, an, the
  • Basic sentence structure: Subject + Verb + Object (I eat rice)
Step 4: Basic Sentence Construction
  • Make simple statements: "I am a student"
  • Ask basic questions: "What is your name?"
  • Use common greetings and responses
  • Practice "to be" verb forms thoroughly
Level 2: Elementary (Pre-Intermediate)
Step 5: Expand Vocabulary (1000-2000 words)
  • Learn thematic vocabulary (food, clothes, transportation, weather)
  • Common phrasal verbs (wake up, give up, look for)
  • Basic prepositions (in, on, at, under, over)
Step 6: More Grammar Structures
  • Past simple tense (I ate, she walked)
  • Future simple with "will" and "going to"
  • Present continuous (I am eating)
  • Question formation (Wh- questions: who, what, when, where, why, how)
  • Negative sentences (I don't like, She didn't go)
Step 7: Reading & Listening Practice
  • Read simple stories and children's books
  • Listen to slow, clear English podcasts
  • Watch English cartoons or simple videos with subtitles
  • Practice understanding context
Level 3: Intermediate
Step 8: Advanced Vocabulary (2000-4000 words)
  • Academic and professional words
  • Idioms and expressions
  • Synonyms and antonyms
  • Word families (create, creation, creative, creator)
Step 9: Complex Grammar
  • Present perfect tense (I have eaten, She has gone)
  • Past continuous (I was eating)
  • Modal verbs (can, could, should, would, must, may, might)
  • Conditional sentences (If I study, I will pass)
  • Passive voice (The book was written by him)
  • Relative clauses (The man who called me)
Step 10: Speaking & Writing Skills
  • Form longer, more complex sentences
  • Write paragraphs with topic sentences
  • Practice describing things, people, and events
  • Join sentences with conjunctions (and, but, because, although)
  • Learn punctuation rules (commas, periods, apostrophes)
Step 11: Reading Comprehension
  • Read articles, short stories, news
  • Understand main ideas and supporting details
  • Practice inference and critical reading
Level 4: Upper-Intermediate
Step 12: Sophisticated Vocabulary (4000-6000 words)
  • Field-specific terminology (for tech/development work)
  • Advanced idioms and collocations
  • Formal vs informal language
  • Business English basics
Step 13: Advanced Grammar Concepts
  • All perfect tenses (past perfect, future perfect)
  • Perfect continuous tenses
  • Advanced conditionals (second, third conditionals)
  • Reported speech (He said that...)
  • Complex sentence structures with multiple clauses
  • Inversion and emphasis
Step 14: Fluency Development
  • Practice speaking naturally without translating in your head
  • Improve pronunciation and intonation
  • Reduce your accent (if desired)
  • Learn connected speech and linking
Step 15: Writing Enhancement
  • Write essays with clear structure (introduction, body, conclusion)
  • Business emails and formal letters
  • Creative writing
  • Learn transition words and phrases
Level 5: Advanced
Step 16: Mastery Vocabulary (6000-10000+ words)
  • Nuanced vocabulary with subtle differences
  • Advanced academic vocabulary
  • Literary and figurative language
  • Native-like expressions
Step 17: Refined Grammar & Style
  • Master all tenses and aspects perfectly
  • Understand subjunctive mood
  • Advanced passive constructions
  • Cleft sentences for emphasis
  • Ellipsis and substitution
Step 18: Professional Communication
  • Business presentations
  • Negotiation language
  • Technical writing (valuable for development work)
  • Academic writing
  • Public speaking
Step 19: Cultural Fluency
  • Understand cultural references
  • Master humor, sarcasm, and irony
  • Learn regional variations (American, British, Australian English)
  • Understand slang and colloquialisms
Step 20: Continuous Improvement
  • Read novels, newspapers, research papers
  • Watch movies and TV shows without subtitles
  • Engage in debates and discussions
  • Write regularly (blog, journal, articles)
  • Think in English

üí° Key Tips for Success

  1. Practice every single day without exception
  2. Don't be afraid to make mistakes - they help you learn
  3. Use English in your daily life (change phone language to English)
  4. Think in English, don't translate from your native language
  5. Be patient - language learning takes time and consistency
  6. Focus on communication, not perfection at first
  7. Since you're learning web development, try reading documentation and tutorials in English - this will help both your English and your career!
