Your Step-by-Step Guide from Basic to Advanced Mathematics

Foundation Level (Grades 1-5)

1. Number Sense & Operations

  • Counting, place value, comparing numbers
  • Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
  • Fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Factors, multiples, prime numbers
  • Order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)

2. Basic Geometry

  • Shapes: triangles, squares, circles, rectangles
  • Perimeter and area of basic shapes
  • Lines, angles, and their types
  • Symmetry and transformations

3. Measurement

  • Units of length, weight, volume, time
  • Converting between units
  • Reading scales and graphs

Intermediate Level (Grades 6-8)

4. Pre-Algebra

  • Integers and rational numbers
  • Ratios, proportions, and rates
  • Introduction to variables and expressions
  • Solving simple equations
  • Introduction to coordinate plane

5. Advanced Fractions & Decimals

  • Operations with fractions and decimals
  • Percentages in real-world problems
  • Powers and exponents
  • Square roots and cube roots

6. Geometry Expansion

  • Volume and surface area (cubes, cylinders, spheres)
  • Pythagorean theorem
  • Circle properties (circumference, area)
  • Basic trigonometry introduction

7. Statistics & Probability

  • Mean, median, mode, range
  • Data representation (graphs, charts)
  • Basic probability concepts
  • Sample spaces and outcomes

High School Level (Grades 9-12)

8. Algebra I

  • Linear equations and inequalities
  • Systems of equations
  • Polynomials (adding, subtracting, multiplying)
  • Factoring techniques
  • Graphing linear functions
  • Word problems and applications

9. Geometry

  • Proofs and logical reasoning
  • Congruence and similarity
  • Triangles (properties, theorems)
  • Quadrilaterals and polygons
  • Circles (arcs, chords, tangents)
  • 3D geometry and spatial reasoning

10. Algebra II

  • Quadratic equations and functions
  • Exponential and logarithmic functions
  • Rational expressions and equations
  • Radical expressions
  • Complex numbers
  • Sequences and series

11. Trigonometry

  • Trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, tan)
  • Unit circle and radian measure
  • Trigonometric identities
  • Solving trigonometric equations
  • Law of sines and cosines
  • Applications in real-world problems

12. Pre-Calculus

  • Functions and their properties
  • Polynomial and rational functions
  • Conic sections (parabolas, ellipses, hyperbolas)
  • Matrices and determinants
  • Vectors and parametric equations
  • Limits (introduction)

University/Advanced Level

13. Calculus I (Differential Calculus)

  • Limits and continuity
  • Derivatives and differentiation rules
  • Applications of derivatives (optimization, rates of change)
  • Related rates problems
  • Curve sketching

14. Calculus II (Integral Calculus)

  • Indefinite and definite integrals
  • Integration techniques
  • Applications of integrals (area, volume, work)
  • Sequences and series
  • Taylor and Maclaurin series

15. Calculus III (Multivariable Calculus)

  • Partial derivatives
  • Multiple integrals
  • Vector calculus
  • Line and surface integrals
  • Green's, Stokes', and Divergence theorems

16. Linear Algebra

  • Matrices and matrix operations
  • Systems of linear equations
  • Vector spaces and subspaces
  • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
  • Linear transformations
  • Applications in computer science and data science

17. Differential Equations

  • First-order differential equations
  • Second-order linear equations
  • Systems of differential equations
  • Laplace transforms
  • Applications in physics and engineering

18. Discrete Mathematics

  • Logic and proof techniques
  • Set theory
  • Combinatorics and counting
  • Graph theory
  • Number theory
  • Algorithms and complexity

19. Probability & Statistics

  • Probability distributions
  • Random variables
  • Expected value and variance
  • Hypothesis testing
  • Regression analysis
  • Statistical inference

20. Abstract Algebra (Optional Advanced)

  • Groups, rings, and fields
  • Homomorphisms and isomorphisms
  • Galois theory

21. Real Analysis (Optional Advanced)

  • Rigorous treatment of limits and continuity
  • Sequences and series
  • Metric spaces
  • Measure theory

Learning Tips

  • Practice daily - Even 30 minutes consistently beats occasional long sessions
  • Master each level before moving on - Don't skip fundamentals
  • Solve lots of problems - Math is learned by doing, not just reading
  • Use multiple resources - Khan Academy, YouTube, Brilliant.org
  • Focus on understanding WHY, not just HOW - Understand concepts deeply
  • Apply to programming - Use your coding skills to visualize math concepts
  • Join online communities - Reddit's r/learnmath, Stack Exchange
